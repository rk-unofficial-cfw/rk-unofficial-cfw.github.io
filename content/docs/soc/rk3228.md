The RK3228 is an ultra-low-cost SoC designed for basic 1080p media boxes and set-top devices. Mainline kernel support is minimal — most devices shipped with a very old vendor BSP. Community bring-up is in early stages.

## Available Firmware

<div class="fw-entry fw-entry--libreelec">
  <div class="fw-entry-header">
    <span class="fw-pill fw-pill--libreelec">LibreELEC</span>
    <span class="fw-entry-status">Stable</span>
  </div>
  <p class="fw-entry-desc">
    LibreELEC supports RK322x-based media boxes (RK3228 and RK3229). Hardware H.264 and H.265 decode via the vendor VPU driver is functional. Images boot from both SD card and eMMC. The RK3229 variant (used in many no-brand TV boxes) is fully compatible with the same images.

RK3229 is a pin-compatible die-shrink of RK3228 with minor clock differences. Both chips are supported by the same firmware images and device trees.
